Williams Given 4-Game Suspension
Sunday, May 29th, 2050
The league has issued a 4-game suspension to Admirals second baseman Tony Williams for his bad conduct yesterday at the Waterford North-Lake Mills game at White Knights Ballpark. Williams was tossed out of the game for heatedly questioning a third-strike call. Once the home plate umpire ejected him, Williams ran over to argue more, even though the umpire warned him specifically, "Don't you come over here."

Williams then retreated to the dugout, where he threw several bats onto the field and followed with a toss of an athletic trainer's bag. Later, Williams ran out of the clubhouse tunnel to continue arguing -- a big no-no for a player who has already been thrown out.

Williams is hitting .137 so far this season with 7 RBIs and 4 runs scored. He has 16 hits including no home runs, 9 walks and .197 OBP.
