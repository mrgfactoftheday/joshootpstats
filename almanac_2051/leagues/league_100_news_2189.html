Another Setback for Dunnam, Forced To Retire
Wednesday, October 19th, 2050
A tearful Eric Dunnam stood with Dodge Fighting Scots officials and teammates as he announced that his baseball career was over. The 39-year-old starting pitcher was recovering from a torn labrum, but the injury recently became worse and doctors recommended that he retire from the game. "It's really hard to leave because I love to play baseball," Dunnam explained to reporters. "I'll miss playing baseball and living out my childhood dream, but I'll always cherish the memories I have. Despite this bad break, life has been good to me."

Over the course of his career, Dunnam has a won-lost record of 14 wins and 2 losses with 178 strikeouts in 198.1 innings pitched with a lifetime 1.63 ERA.
