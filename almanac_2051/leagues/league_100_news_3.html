Snider Joins Greats on 5th Attempt
Wednesday, January 5th, 2050
There is a new statue in the Wisconsin Baseball League Hall of Fame.

It belongs Tom Snider. The star pitcher was inducted this past weekend. All it took to get there was 106 wins.

"I always dreamed of being a Wisconsin Baseball League player, but I never dreamed of being in the Hall of Fame," Snider said, "I could have never envisioned myself standing next to my childhood idols and yet here I am. And to the Hall of Famers sitting behind me, I am truly humbled and in awe of standing here in front of you."

In his career, Tom Snider posted a record of 106-46 and fanned 1383 batters in 1533.2 innings with a 1.43 ERA.

At times Tom Snider fought back tears as he thanked his family and close friends for sticking by him when he had to overcome some difficult personal problems.

Summing up his years on the diamond, Snider offered a hopeful message to the large induction crowd.

"I care for this game with my heart and soul. I dedicated my life to being the best pitcher I could be. You leave me humbled and grateful for this honor. I'd like to leave an offering of a message of hope. That is, with the grace of God, you can change your life, whoever you are."
