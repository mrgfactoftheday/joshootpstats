Wisconsin Baseball League Honors Alcaraz
Wednesday, January 5th, 2050
Today was a historic day that Jordan Alcaraz will never forget. The superb pitcher was inducted into the Wisconsin Baseball League Hall of Fame alongside the great baseball immortals of the past.

He was introduced by one of his former managers, who said, "He was the best I have seen in my lifetime. Jordan was a fabulous pitcher. The key to his success was that he threw the ball as far from the bat and as close to the plate as possible."

The talented pitcher retired from baseball at 40, but not before he amassed 131 victories. In his illustrious career, Jordan Alcaraz was 131-95 with a 1.72 ERA. He made 386 mound appearances and struck out 1749 in 2114.2 innings.

Alcaraz brought down the house with his closing remarks. "This is really a sad day for me. I've never had a job. I've always played baseball. I guess I'll have to go to work now."
