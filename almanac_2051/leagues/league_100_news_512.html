Walker Spins Perfect Game Against Surf
Monday, June 13th, 2050
With the final, flawless out recorded, the Washburn Prospects piled onto Trent Walker at the center of the Surf Ballpark diamond. Walker joined baseball's most hallowed club today, completing a perfect game against the Calumet Surf.

The 28-year-old left-hander kept the Calumet offense guessing all day long and with some help from the defenders behind him, etched his name into the record books with the 1-0 shutout.

"His breaking pitches had more movement than I've ever seen from Trent," Washburn manager Jose Rivera said in the postgame interview. "He made everyone's job easy today. He just saw a target and he hit it. Simple as that."

For the game, Walker retired all 27 batters he faced and fanned 9 of them. He walked none and nobody got on base.

His record this year is 5-1 in 6 starts and Walker has a 0.41 ERA.
