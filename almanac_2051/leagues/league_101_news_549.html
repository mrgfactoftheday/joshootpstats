Suspensions Handed Out for Bench-Clearing Brawl
Sunday, June 19th, 2050
After an intense confrontation, both the Phoenix Herman Marshall and the Wild Things Jason Obando were handed their punishments today. The league commissioner's office issued suspensions for an on-the-field conflict that occurred at a recent West Milwaukee-Evergreen game. Herman Marshall will miss 5 games, while Jason Obando was suspended for 10 games.

"Sometimes we go too far and have to pay the consequences," Herman Marshall commented later. "Hopefully cooler heads will prevail the next time we play."

In 15 games this season Marshall has batted at a .211 pace with 8 hits and no home runs.

This season Obando has chalked up a 1.42 ERA while ringing up 5 saves with 3 wins and 2 losses. He has made 31 relief appearances, pitching 38 innings, giving up 18 hits, and holding opponents to a .141 batting average. Obando has also recorded 19 strikeouts and allowed 14 walks.
