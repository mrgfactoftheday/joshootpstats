McGowen Unhittable in Perfect Game
Sunday, July 10th, 2050
Over the course of baseball history, some moments stand a little taller than others. One of those moments happened today at Wallworth Fields.

Wallworth right-hander John McGowen threw a perfect game at as the Thunder Cats beat the Vernon Browns, 1-0. In his post-game comments, McGowen told reporters that it was something he had never dreamed of.

"As a kid, you always think of pitching to win the final game of a championship," McGowen said. "You never think of pitching a perfect game, because it just seems unattainable. I'll remember this day for the rest of my life."

By the end of the game, McGowen had rung up 10 Vernon hitters on strikeouts, and did not allow a single baserunner. The fans gave McGowen a standing ovation as he left the mound, knowing they had witnessed one of the rarest events in the sport.

On the year McGowen has worked 30.2 innings with 30 strikeouts en route to a 2-0 record and 1.76 ERA.
