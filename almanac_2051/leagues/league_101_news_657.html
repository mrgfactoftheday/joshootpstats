Trouble in Black River Falls, Lee Suffers Setback
Thursday, June 30th, 2050
"It's worse than we thought." That was the news from the Maroons clubhouse today as it was learned that starting pitcher Kyung-hwan Lee will be out for an added 6 days as a result of a recent injury. His treatment for back spasms will have to be extended. Initially it didn't seem the injury was a major concern, but the Black River Falls starting pitcher told team trainers of some discomfort yesterday morning, and upon examination it was determined to be a more serious injury. "We'd rather take this thing slowly and make sure Kyung-hwan gets back onto the field healthy," said the team's head trainer.

This season Lee has worked 68.1 innings with 13 strikeouts en route to a 3-6 record and a 2.37 ERA.
