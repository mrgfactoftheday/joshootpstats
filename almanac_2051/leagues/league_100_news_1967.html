Sunday, August 14th, 2050
The Columbia Lakers were just hoping for a solid outing from Nick Davis. Instead, they got something special.

The 31-year-old hurler tossed a no-hitter against the Waukesha Capitals, helping the Lakers take the 1-0 win.

"I felt good throughout the game," he said after his spectacular outing for the Lakers. "As I was going into the ninth inning, I wasn't nervous. I knew I had a chance to do something special."

Davis's repertoire has a knuckle curve, fastball, and changeup, and he used it all to full advantage, keeping the Waukesha hitters off balance all game long. The Columbia hurler fanned 7 and walked 2 in his history-making performance.

"His knuckle curve looked about the size of a watermelon seed and it hissed at you as it passed," Waukesha manager Sean Mowery joked after the game.

To date this season Davis has compiled a 7-6 record with a 1.75 ERA in 17 starts.
