Creed Spins Masterpiece, No-Hits Ladysmith
Friday, June 10th, 2050
The no-hitter is one of baseball's truly egalitarian achievements. It's done by the great and the not-so-great alike, and for nearly all of them it represents the pinnacle of a career. Delafield Steelheads pitcher Joe Creed had his moment today at Steelheads Ballpark, holding the Ladysmith Scots hitless and beating them, 9-0.

"Everything was working for him," Scots manager Sam Rhodes said. "He had a lot of late movement that seemed to cause the guys trouble, especially from the fifth inning on. He was in command."

Creed rung up 14 strikeouts, issued 1 walk and faced only 28 hitters in his historic outing. After the game, he spoke to BNN reporters in the Delafield locker room.

"I really got nervous in the last inning," Creed recalled. "I knew I had a chance at a no-hitter but it was hard to stay calm and concentrate. Thank goodness for my catcher. He told me he would do all the thinking and I could just concentrate on hitting his glove."
