Great Year Gets Severance NC Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, October 21st, 2050
At the age of 25, Steve Severance of the Rusk Firebirds could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 2050 was good enough to win the second baseman the Northern Counties Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .244 batting average this year, as well as a .360 on-base percentage. He played 100 games and racked up 89 hits, 19 doubles, 4 triples, 17 home runs, 44 RBIs and 45 runs scored.

He received 52 first place votes out of a possible 72. Dave Huerta of the Price Penguins finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Danny Hernandez of the St. Croix Blue Sox finished third with 11 first place votes.
